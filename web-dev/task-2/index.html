Using semantic HTML in a webpage

The <header> introduces the page's topic and context.

It contains the main headings and information that must be present at the top of the page.
It could be also used for navbars.

The <main> tag contains the primary content of the document.

It holds the central and most important information of the webpage, excluding headers, footers, and sidebars.

The <section> tag groups related articles.

It groups related content together, usually under a common theme or heading within the main structure.

The <article> tag represents independent, self-contained pieces of content.

It represents independent, self-contained content such as a news story, blog post, or individual topic entry.

The <footer> tag provides author and assignment details.

It defines the closing section of a webpage, often containing credits, contact info, or legal details.
